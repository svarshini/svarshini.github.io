About

I am a Research Assistant at the Department of Computer Science, Indian Institute of Science (IISc), advised by Prof. Vijay Natarajan. Having received my Bachelors in Mechanical Engineering from Manipal Institute of Technology (2018), my thirst to contribute to technology nudged me towards computational and algorithmic domains of research and I have been fascinated ever since. My current areas of research include algorithms, parallel computing, computational geometry, topology and graph theory.

Prior to this, I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Ramsharan Rangarajan, Dept of Mechanical Engg, IISc (Jan-Aug 2018) where I worked on mesh optimization, adaptive mesh refinement and parallel computing. I also delved into experimental research (May-July 2017) under the guidance of Prof. Arindrajit Chowdhury at IIT Bombay.

I enjoy conflating research with social causes that make a difference. I am a member of the Coronavirus Visualization Team, which is a student-run non-profit from Harvard University which aims to eliminate the overwhelming influx of misinformation during the COVID-19 pandemic, with the aid of visualizations, data analysis and research. I am primarily involved as the Project Planning Co-Director, Project Co-Lead for the 'Global Initiative to End Gender-Based Violence' and the Community Manager for Social Media.

When I am not thinking about computational problems, I marvel at literature, music and astrophysics. Some of my work is listed below. Click on the links to see more details.
